Techie Stuff

So, let's be real - this wouldn't be a Zach's Christmas List without some tech gadgets on it. You know me, and you expect such. Surprisingly, it's actually a relatively small and (gasp) inexpensive list. So let's get to it!

First, I've listed some things that have to do with graphic design. As someone who's getting into this whole building websites thing, I kind of would like to be able to fully control all aspects of how I build my sites, from the graphic design all the way to the databases. Most of the programming stuff I can handle, since I've got lots of free software from UofL, but where I'm lacking is tools to get me started in graphic design. Enter my first wishes:

Graphic Design Stuff

Adobe Creative Cloud

Adobe sells the top design software in the world. I'm sure you've heard of Photoshop, right? Well yeah, it's not just a thing that everyone has, it's a pretty expensive bit of software that actually has lots of accompanying software that makes it even more useful. I used to think that I'd have to just buy each individual product separately, but then I learned about the Creative Cloud subscription. Basically, for a decent amount of money per month, you can have access to any and all of their products (which is a crazy-good deal). Best of all, students get insane discounts on that subscription (and my wife and I just so happen to both be students!).

P.S. There are two different versions of the subscription that I'm asking for: 1-month subscriptions and 12-month subscriptions. The price that I'm listing is for the 12-month subscription (because the more months I can have to test it out, the better), but 1-month subscriptions are always cool too!

$239.99

Wacom Drawing Tablet

Believe it or not, it's actually somewhat difficult to be artistic on a computer with a mouse. I could show you a picture of things I've had to quickly draw on my computer with my mouse, but nobody needs you throwing up all over the place. So, instead of dropping some G's on a touchscreen tablet, I've opted for a much-cheaper option: a drawing tablet. Essentially, it's like a giant touchpad, except it comes with a drawing pen that has some thousand levels of sensitivity, making it an incredibly-close representation of real-life drawing. All-in-all, this is another one of those must-have kinds of things if I really want to get into graphic design at all.

P.S. There's not a specific tablet that I want. The Wacom brand is supposed to be really nice, and they obviously have some models that are way nicer than others, but just use your discretion. I mean, I don't need a the mac-daddy tablet, but the crappiest model you can find won't really do me much good either. Ya feel me?

$89.99

Alright, time to do things a little differently. Danielle and I have really gotten into Netflix. We probably watch TV shows on there as much as we actually use DirecTV, surprisingly. However, we only have one device that can actually play Netflix, and it's in our bedroom. While that's all nice and fun for watching TV before bed, it makes things kinda tough when we want to watch some TV while we're eating dinner or something. Enter the three devices you see above.

Each device has its advantages and disadvantages. For example, the Chromecast is made by Google and it's at least $65 less expensive than either of the other two options, but it also doesn't have nearly as many applications that work with it (pretty much just Netflix, Youtube and Pandora), and it can't do anything like play DVDs. A Blu-Ray Player can do all of those extra things, but it still doesn't have that many apps and it's decently expensive. And lastly, the Roku 3 is probably the most full-featured device on the list, with everything from Netflix to HBO Go to sports channels to Angry Birds (yup, you can play a few games on it), but it's pretty darn expensive.

Obviously, I'm not asking for all three - just one! I don't really have any preference either, I'd just be happy with any of them :)
